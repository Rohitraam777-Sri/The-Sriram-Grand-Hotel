About The Dish

Traditional South Indian breakfast spread — vibrant, aromatic, and comforting. Here’s what’s in the setup: Idli – soft, steamed rice cakes garnished with coriander. Podi idli – idlis coated with spiced chutney powder (gunpowder) and drizzled with ghee for extra flavor. Dosa – crisp and golden, seen in the background, another South Indian favorite. Sambar – a bowl of spicy lentil and vegetable curry, topped with coriander and red chili. Coconut chutney – creamy white chutney made from coconut and tempered with mustard seeds. Tomato chutney – tangy and spicy red chutney served alongside. Idli podi (spice powder) – dry chutney mix served with ghee or oil. A cup of hot filter coffee (chai-style glass) – the perfect South Indian beverage to complete the meal. Banana chips – crispy snacks, often enjoyed as a side. It’s a hearty and traditional South Indian breakfast platter — a perfect mix of flavors, textures, and aromas.
